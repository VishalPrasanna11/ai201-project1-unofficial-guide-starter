Northeastern University N.U.in Spring Housing

Welcome N.U.in Students

Housing Statistics
- Historically, NUin students placed into 39 different residence halls
- Over 85% of NUin students placed into their top housing style choice or with requested roommate
- Average placements: 10% apartment style housing, 85% semi-private suites, 5% traditional first-year halls
- Of those in apartment style housing: 50% in Standard or Economy rate buildings, 50% in Enhanced rate buildings
- Resident Assistant to student ratio: 1:40 across campus
- All preference forms reviewed in enrollment deposit date order; accommodations made based on availability

Program Overview
Students participating in N.U.in Program during fall semester return to Boston Campus university housing in January. Unlike fall entry first years, N.U.in students can live in any housing style while participating in their top choice Living Learning Community. Environments provide opportunities to learn outside classroom through Living Learning Community program.

NUin students required to live in University Housing for their first and second year (same as fall entry first years).

How to Apply

Timeline
- Housing Application Preference Form Available: Late October
- Housing Accommodation Requests Completed by: Early November
- Housing Preference Form Due: Early December
- Assignments sent via Northeastern email: Mid December

Process
NUin students receive information about applying for Spring housing in October. Students indicate housing priorities through preference form. Assignments based on preferences in enrollment deposit date order. Assignments sent to Husky email account starting mid-to-late December.

Housing Types to Expect

Placement Overview
Most incoming first years in Fall placed in traditional or Semi-Private Suite Style residence halls. N.U.in students placed throughout campus in all housing styles. Housing options may be similar or different from current experience.

Note: Cannot guarantee first choice placement. All information provided in application used to make best housing assignment possible for each NUin student. All housing applications reviewed in enrollment deposit date order; accommodations made based on availability.

All N.U.in students required to have meal plan.

Living Learning Communities (LLCs)

In addition to building style preference, students rank their top choice Living Learning Communities. To make transition to campus more seamless and better integrate returning NUin students into existing first-year community on Boston Campus, all NUin students can participate in Living Learning Communities.

Regardless of assignment to traditional first-year housing or apartment/suites with older students, all NUin students become members of their top choice LLC and invited to participate in their programming.

As LLC member, students become connected virtually and invited to attend events and initiatives affiliated with LLC.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I choose my residence hall?
Students provide preferences for housing type (apartment style, suite style, traditional corridor style), not specific residence halls. Last year's NUin cohort placed into 39 different residence halls.

When is move-in?
Spring Move-in in early January, takes place over several days based on assignment. Specific move-in dates and times sent to students with room assignment in December, including information about requesting date change.

When will I know my housing assignment?
Most NUin students receive assignments mid to late December.

What should I bring when I move in?
Bring needed items; leave prohibited and extra items at home. Specific items listed on website and Guide to Residence Hall Living. Communicate with roommate(s) beforehand to avoid duplicates.

How do I reserve a Microfridge or ship to my room before move in?
Information available on vendors page.
