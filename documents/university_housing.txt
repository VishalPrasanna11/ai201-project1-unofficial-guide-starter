Northeastern University Housing in Boston

Overview

Northeastern University's Housing & Residential Life system includes student housing owned or leased by the University. Students must conduct themselves consistent with University expectations stated in Guide to Residence Hall Living, Student Handbook, Residence Hall and Dining License Agreement, and all applicable University policies, procedures, supplemental agreements, rules and regulations.

Standard Room Features
- Bed frame
- Mattress (twin XL)
- Desk
- Desk chair
- Dresser

Note: Each room is unique; square footage/measurements of individual rooms not provided.

General Housing Information
- All Northeastern University housing is smoke-free
- Majority are double or triple occupancy rooms
- Dining halls located in Stetson East, Stetson West, and International Village
- Common community spaces available in most halls for studying, group projects, or student activities
- All first-year students required to have meal plan
- Single rooms available on limited basis (indicate preference on housing application or contact Disability Resource Center or University Health and Counseling Services through Housing Accommodation Process for medical/accessibility concerns)
- All Gender housing options available
- University reserves right to alter designations at any time
- During certain terms, first-year students could be assigned to any university residence hall, even those without First Year designation
- NUin students often placed in second-year or upperclassmen accommodations based on availability for Spring of their first year upon return from NUin semester

First Year Students

Housing Requirements
Undergraduates entering as first-year students to Boston through fall admission and NUin will live in university housing during their first and second years, unless global experience or co-op experience takes them outside city of Boston.

First Year Experience (FYE) Program
Developed to help first-year students transition into college life. Residential Life works with students to build strong community living environments supporting personal development and academic success. Environments provide opportunities to learn outside classroom through Living Learning Community program.

Four Pillars of FYE Program:
1. Personal Development
2. Academic Success
3. Experiential Learning
4. Community Development

These pillars are guiding principles of programs and resources specific to first-year student needs. Goal: help students navigate University, familiarize them with living away from home, and adjust to life in new environment.

Required Second Year Students

Housing Requirements
Currently, undergraduates entering as first-year students to Boston through fall admission and NUin required to live in university housing for first two years.

Beginning Fall 2025: Students entering as first-year students to Boston or Oakland through fall admission and NUin program required to live in university housing for first two years.

Special Programs
- NU Immerse or Foundation Year students: Refer to transfer student information for second year in Boston
- NYC Scholars and London Scholars: Refer to Non-Required Information ahead of first year in Boston

University reserves right to alter designations at any time throughout year. NUin students often placed in second-year or upperclassmen accommodations based on availability for Spring of their first year upon return from NUin semester.

Non-Required Upperclassmen Students

University housing for non-required undergraduates (for 2026-2027 includes students completing London Scholars and New York Scholars first-year programs coming to Boston Campus) is limited and on space-available basis. These students live in mix of university and off-campus housing in Boston or participate in co-op, study abroad, or learning opportunities outside Boston.

University reserves right to alter designations at any time throughout year. NUin students often placed in second-year or upperclassmen accommodations based on availability for Spring of their first year upon return from NUin semester.

Transfer Students

Housing Availability
University housing for transfer students and students in third year and beyond is limited and on space-available basis. These students live in mix of university and off-campus housing in Boston or participate in co-op, study abroad, or learning opportunities outside Boston.

Currently, university housing not available for transfer students being admitted for upcoming academic year. No transfer student waitlist exists. New transfer students informed of housing availability at time of admission.
