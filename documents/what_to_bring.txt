Northeastern University What To Bring

University-Provided Items
University provides each student with:
- Bed
- Desk
- Chair
- Dresser
- Window shade

This applies to all housing types: traditional residence halls, suite-style accommodations, and apartments.

Note: Some double and triple rooms have only one closet to share with roommates.

Packing Recommendations
- Use list as guideline and choose what is essential
- Communicate with roommate to avoid duplicates
- Do not over-pack
- Additional items can be shipped after start of term rush
- Recommended: Pack in duffel bag style options that can be folded for storing (space in room will be limited; these bags can remain for repacking when departing)

Boston Climate
Early September has hot days, but Fall climate in Boston cools quickly. Average high temperatures drop from 73°F to 62°F by October.

What You May Want to Bring

Clothing

General:
- Socks & Underwear
- Shorts, Pants, Skirts
- Shirts (short and long sleeves)
- Sweatshirts/Sweaters
- Jackets, Hat, Gloves
- Sleepwear
- Athleticwear

Footwear (2-3 pairs):
- Water resistant
- Everyday
- Athletic
- Flip-flops

Essentials
- Computer and cables
- Power Outlet Strip with Surge Protector
- Phone and charger
- Headphones
- Backpack and Laundry bag (Pack them for Move-in)
- 2-3 Towels & Washcloths
- Umbrella and/or Raincoat
- 1-2 Sets of Twin XL Sheets and 1-2 Pillows
- 1 Twin XL Blanket or Comforter
- Fan
- Hangers (recommend thin style)
- Single-serve snacks
- U-Lock (for bike storage)

Medicine and Toiletries
- Bathroom caddy or carrying bag
- Prescription and Over-the-Counter Medicines
- Comb/brush
- Shampoo, Conditioner, Body and Face Wash
- Toothbrush, Toothpaste, Floss
- Cosmetics and Deodorant
- Q-tips
- Oral Thermometer
- Lotion/Sunscreen
- Menstrual products
- Razors, tweezers, and nail clippers
- Eyeglasses and sunglasses
- Contact lenses and solutions
- Hair dryer
- Hand sanitizer

Cleaning Products

For Suite and Apartments (Coordinate with all occupants):
- General Bathroom Cleaner and Sponges
- Toilet Cleaner/Brush
- Toilet Paper

For All Students:
- Laundry detergent and dryer sheets
- Disinfectant wipes
- Swiffer or small vacuum

Microwave and Refrigerator
In all traditional and suite-style accommodations, students not permitted to bring own microwave. If wishing to have microwave, may rent micro-fridge prior to moving to campus. For information about renting micro-fridge, visit Vendor Information page.

What NOT to Bring

Not comprehensive list. See Guide to Residence Hall Living for more details. Roommates may want to decide together on what to bring, either in advance or shopping together after move-in.

Prohibited Items:
- No outside furniture allowed (only exception: desk chair)
- Any appliance with heating coil/uncontained element
- Tapestry wall hangings
- Air conditioning units
- Halogen lamps
- Weightlifting equipment
